At the commencement of the argument, all parties agreed to conduct a composite

hearing of all the connected cases. Hence, this judgment must apply to the cases

bearing numbers SC/FR/59/2020, SC/FR/60/2020, SC/FR/61/2020, and

The Petitioners in all the above cases are Senior Superintendents of Police at the time

they had filed these cases. In these Applications the Petitioners have challenged the

RTM 1059 dated 27-01-2020 by which the Inspector General of Police has announced

the backdating of promotions granted to three persons. These three persons are:

(i). Superintendent of Police B. M. A. Bandaranayake

(ii). Assistant Superintendent of Police A. R. P. J. De Alwis

(iii). Superintendent of Police Manoj Ranagala.

(ii) then to the rank of Senior Superintendent of Police with effect from 01-01-

2018.

The Petitioners in their Petitions have named as Respondents, those three Police

Police Officers as 12th to 14th Respondents; in SC/FR/60/2020 as 10th to 12th

Let me at this juncture, turn to the basis upon which the Petitioners have challenged

the said backdating of the promotions granted to the 12th to 14th Respondents as per

Petitioners of each of those four cases are different, I will proceed to first set out

In SC/FR/59/2020, the Petitioners as well as the 12th to 14th Respondents had

The plain reading of the above text shows clearly, two reasons which can be taken as

the basis for the decision contained therein. These two reasons can be listed as

follows:

(i) The National Police Commission having considered the Final Order

pronounced by the Supreme Court in SC/FR/125/2009, has approved the

the proposed action of backdating the promotions of the afore-said three

Police Officers;

(ii) It did so as those three Police Officers were the Petitioners of that case i.e.,

SC/FR/125/2009; this also indicates that the Supreme Court in

SC/FR/125/2009 has pronounced its Final Order in favour of its Petitioners

(the three Police Officers).

However, contrary to the above reasoning, the Chairman of the National Police

Commission in his affidavit dated 31-07-2025 has categorically denied that the action

of backdating the promotions of the 12th to 14th Respondents as per the RTM 1059

dated 27-01-2020 (P2) was carried out based on the Final Order in SC/FR/125/2009.

The Chairman of the NPC has positively asserted this position in Paragraph 16 of his

affidavit dated 31-07-2025, which I will reproduce below:

“Answering the averments contained in Paragraphs 22 and 23 of the said

decision of the NPC had not been based on the same.”

I observe that the position of the Chairman of the National Police Commission has also

been clearly set out in 8th and 15th Paragraphs of his affidavit dated 31-07-2025. It is

his position that that “...the ante-dation of the promotions of the 12th, 13th and 14th

Respondents had been granted after an impartial, unbiased and after a very careful

consideration of the facts and circumstances of each of their cases, with the

Moreover, in his affidavit dated 31-07-2025, the Chairman of the National Police

Commission has stated that the 12th to 14th Respondents remained promoted as Chief

Inspectors of Police with effect from 01-01-2006, but continuously challenged their

non-appointment as Chief Inspectors of Police with effect from 01-01-2003, the date

they believed they should have been appointed to that post.5 0The date 01-01-2003

Inspectors of Police.

(P2), the approval of the backdating of the promotions of the 12th to 14th Respondents

was done by the National Police Commission on the strength of the Final Order

pronounced by the Supreme Court in SC/FR/125/2009.

Let me now turn to the case SC/FR/125/2009. The Petitioners have submitted a copy

of the Final Order made by this Court in the case SC/FR/125/2009. That is the Order

dated 19-05-2014 which states as follows:

“Learned Deputy Solicitor General informs Court that the Petitioners have been

promoted to the rank of Assistant Superintendent of Police with effect from

13.01.2014.”

In view of these promotions, these proceedings are terminated.”

Thus, it is clear from the above Order, that the Supreme Court in SC/FR/125/2009 has

not pronounced its Final Order in favour of its Petitioners (the 12th to 14th

Respondents) as claimed by P2.

I further observe that while the above Order of the Supreme Court in SC/FR/125/2009

was pronounced on 19-05-2014, the National Police Commission has decided to back

date the promotions of 12th to 14th Respondents in 2020. This is manifest from the

letter dated 12-01-2020 sent to the Inspector General of Police by the NPC, 6 and the

minutes of the NPC on 16-01-2020.7 I must also mention here that the said minutes

of the NPC on 16-01-2020 has merely recorded only the decision without assigning

any reason for the said decision.

Let me now consider the arguments relied upon by the learned Counsel on behalf of

the 12th to 14th Respondents. They state that Sri Lanka Police took steps to

amalgamate two lists: one containing Special Task Force and the other containing

Police regular Service. This was on the eve of making promotions to the rank of Chief

single list they were placed lower than they should have been on the list of seniority

of the Police regular Service if the lists had been kept separate. It is as a result of the

above loss of seniority in the said created single list that the 12th to 14th Respondents

have complained that they failed to secure the promotions to the rank of Chief

Inspector of Police in 2004.

Admittedly, a number of other Inspectors of Police were aggrieved and such aggrieved

persons had filed cases in this Court to challenge their non-promotion. Case bearing

No. SC/FR/600/2003 is said to be one such case where one of the 12th to 14th

Respondents had stood as the 1st Petitioner.

However, the 12th to 14th Respondents during the pendency of those cases received

promotions as Chief Inspectors of Police with effect from 01-01-2006. This was on the

basis of their period of service based on a general scheme of promotion introduced as

a policy.

According to the 12th to 14th Respondents, they were also called for a fresh interview

but were not successful that time as well. The 12th to 14th Respondents attribute their

failure this time for the NPC’s failure to award them due marks. Admittedly they took

this complain to the Administrative Appeals Tribunal but failed to get any order in their

favour.

The 12th to 14th Respondents complain that they lost their eligibility to apply for the

promotions to the rank of Assistant Superintendent of Police in 2008 batch due to the

same reason: i.e., due to their being not promoted as Chief Inspector of Police with

effect from 01-01-2003.

According to the 12th to 14th Respondents, they filed SC/FR/125/2009 in the Supreme

Court to challenge the action of the Inspector General of Police in not calling them for

the interview for the promotion to the rank of Assistant Superintendent of Police in

2008 batch. In SC/FR/125/2009 the order the 12th to 14th Respondents had sought

[SC/FR/59/2020, SC/FR/60/2020, SC/FR/61/2020 & SC/FR/63/2020]

Page 53 of 54 from the Supreme Court was to promote them as Chief Inspector of Police with effect

from 01-01-2003. As has been already mentioned above, this Court has terminated

proceedings subsequent to the promotions of the 12th to 14th Respondents as Assistant

Superintendent of Police (subsequently). Thus, although that was their primary

grievance, the 12th to 14th Respondents had failed to pursue this grievance any longer

in that case.

According to the 12th to 14th Respondents, it was thereafter that a committee

comprising of three: Director Legal; Director HRM; and Director Administration had

recommended the impugned backdating of their promotion. The 12th to 14th

Respondents themselves have stated that one of the members of the three-member

panel now stands as one of the Petitioners in SC/FR/61/2020: i.e., the 3rd Petitioner

in SC/FR/61/2020 who functioned as Director Legal in the afore-said three-member

panel. This assertion itself is a factor indicative of the degree of independence the said

three-member panel had exercised at that time.

The above facts when taken together would only point clearly to the fact that the 12th

to 14th Respondents at several times had taken their grievance to various fora but did

not either succeed or pursued them to the end. Although the Respondents have taken

up the position that the ante-dation of the promotions granted to the 12th to 14th

Respondents was done after an impartial, unbiased and after very careful

consideration of the facts and circumstances of each of their cases, the material they

have placed before Court does not warrant upholding such a view.

I must note that even the Respondents have admitted that the 12th to 14th

Respondents are not the only three persons in this category. If so what was the basis

for the NPC to order backdating the promotions of only these three Officers. There is

no explanation forthcoming from the Respondents. The only justification they put

forward is that this was done pursuant to an impartial, unbiased and after very careful

consideration of the facts and circumstances of each of their cases. I must also

mention here that this ground: i.e., the fact that this was decided after an impartial

inquiry only came for the first time in the affidavit filed by the present Chairman of

[SC/FR/59/2020, SC/FR/60/2020, SC/FR/61/2020 & SC/FR/63/2020]

Page 54 of 54 the NPC. Such basis is not mentioned in the decision of the NPC when it made this

decision on 16-01-2020.

In the above circumstances and for the foregoing reasons, I am unable to uphold the

decision of the Inspector General of Police to backdate several promotions granted to

the 12th to 14th Respondents as per RTM 1059 dated 27-01-2020 (P2).

For the foregoing reasons, I declare that the decision of the National Police

Commission to backdate the promotions granted to the 12th to 14th Respondents and

also the decision of the Inspector General of Police to implement the said decision as

per RTM 1059 dated 27-01-2020 (P2) are illegal and hence are infringing upon the

Fundamental Rights of the Petitioners guaranteed under Article 12(1) of the

Constitution.

I proceed to quash the decision contained in the letter dated 12-01-2020 sent to the

Inspector General of Police by the National Police Commission, and in the minutes of

the National Police Commission on 16-01-2020. I also proceed to quash the decision

of the Inspector General of Police to implement the said decision as per RTM 1059

dated 27-01-2020 (P2).
