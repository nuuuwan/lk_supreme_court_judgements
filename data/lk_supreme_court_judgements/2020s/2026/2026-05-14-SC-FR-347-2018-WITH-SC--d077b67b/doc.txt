ARGUED ON: 20-02-2025

DECIDED ON: : 14-05-2026

P. PADMAN SURASENA, CJ

At the commencement of the argument, all parties agreed to conduct a composite

hearing of all connected cases. Hence, this judgment must apply to the cases bearing

numbers SC/FR/347/2018, SC/FR/387/2018, SC/FR/388/2018, SC/FR/392/2018 and

SC/FR/07/2019.

The 1st Respondent (the then Inspector General of Police) by RTM No. 655 dated 23-

05-2018, called for applications from officers holding the rank of Inspector of Police to

be promoted to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police. The Petitioners have produced

this RTM No. 655 dated 23-05-2018 marked in SC/FR/347/2018 as P5; in

SC/FR/387/2018 as P6; in SC/FR/388/2018 as P6; in SC/FR/392/2018 as P6; and in

SC/FR/07/2019 as P4.

SC/FR/347/2018 with SC/FR/387/2018, SC/FR/388/2018, SC/FR/392/2018 and SC/FR/07/2019 Page 39 of 42

According to the said notice, the officers other than from the Special Task Force and

the Support Services who had the following four qualifications were eligible to apply.

The said four qualifications are as follows:

i. The applicant should be an officer holding the rank of Inspector of Police

ii. The applicant should have been confirmed in the rank of Inspector of Police

iii. The applicant must have had three years satisfactory service as at 01-10-

2017

iv. The applicant must have completed eight years active service in the rank of

Inspector of Police by 01-10-2017.

The 10 Petitioners who were then holding the rank of Inspector of Police in Sri Lanka

Police, responding to the notice (RTM No. 655 dated 23-05-2018), had applied for

the afore-said promotions.

Similarly, the fourteen Inspectors of Police whose names are mentioned in the RTM

855 dated 27-09-2018 along with many other Inspectors of Police, had also applied

as per the said notice for this promotion.

The Petitioners have also produced the marking scheme titled “MARKING SCHEME

FOR PROMOTION TO THE RANK OF CHIEF INSPECTOR UNDER THE MERIT +

SENIORITY PROMOTION SCHEME - 2017 (EXCLUDING STF & SUPPORT SERVICES)”.

The Petitioners have produced this marking scheme marked in SC/FR/347/2018 as

P7; in SC/FR/387/2018 as P7; in SC/FR/388/2018 as P7; in SC/FR/392/2018 as P8;

and in SC/FR/07/2019 as P6.

Thereafter, the Director Human Resources Management acting on behalf of the

Inspector General of Police by RTM No. 335 dated 10-08-2018 called 120 applicants

for the interviews which were to be held on 15-08-2018 and 17-08-2018. The

Petitioners have produced this RTM No. 335 dated 10-08-2018 marked in

SC/FR/347/2018 as P8; in SC/FR/387/2018 as P8; in SC/FR/388/2018 as P8; in

SC/FR/392/2018 as P9; and in SC/FR/07/2019 as P7.

SC/FR/347/2018 with SC/FR/387/2018, SC/FR/388/2018, SC/FR/392/2018 and SC/FR/07/2019 Page 40 of 42

It appears that by this time, the Petitioners had previously filed the case bearing No.

SC/FR/168/2010 in this Court. Thus, the Petitioners presumably being aggrieved by

RTM No. 335 dated 10-08-2018, had obtained a stay order from this Court in the case

bearing No. SC/FR/168/2010. The Petitioners have produced this stay order dated 06-

08-2018 marked: in SC/FR/347/2018 as P9; in SC/FR/387/2018 as P9; in

SC/FR/388/2018 as P9; in SC/FR/392/2018 as P10; and in SC/FR/07/2019 as P8.

Pursuant to the said stay order, the 1st Respondent has issued RTM 436 suspending

the said promotion proceedings as per the RTM No. 655 dated 23-05-2018. The

Petitioners have produced the said RTM 436 dated 13-08-2018 marked: in

SC/FR/347/2018 as P10; in SC/FR/387/2018 as P10; in SC/FR/388/2018 as P10; in

SC/FR/392/2018 as P11; and in SC/FR/07/2019 as P9.

According to the Petitioners, during the period that the afore-said stay order was in

force, the 1st Respondent by RTM 855 dated 27-09-2018 had announced that the

National Police Commission has approved the promotions of the fourteen Inspectors

of Police whose names are mentioned in the said RTM 855, to the rank of Chief

Inspector of Police on supernumerary basis with effect from 02-10-2017. The

Petitioners have produced the said RTM 855 dated 27-09-2018 marked: in

SC/FR/347/2018 as P11; in SC/FR/387/2018 as P11; in SC/FR/388/2018 as P11; in

SC/FR/392/2018 as P12; and in SC/FR/07/2019 as P10.

The Petitioners in their Petitions have stated that they are not aware of the basis on

which the National Police Commission has promoted the said fourteen Inspectors of

Police as per the said RTM 855. The Petitioners in their Petition have also stated that

the promotions of the said fourteen Inspectors of Police as per the said RTM 855 has

been carried out contrary to the afore-mentioned marking scheme.

It is on the above basis that the Petitioners have challenged the promotions to the

rank of Chief Inspector of Police, granted to those fourteen Inspectors of Police as per

the said RTM 855.

SC/FR/347/2018 with SC/FR/387/2018, SC/FR/388/2018, SC/FR/392/2018 and SC/FR/07/2019 Page 41 of 42

The Petitioners in their Petitions have identified those fourteen Inspectors of Police

who were promoted to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police as per the said RTM 855

as follows. In SC/FR/347/2018 they are named as 10th to 23rd Respondents; in

SC/FR/387/2018 as 10th to 23rd Respondents; in SC/FR/388/2018 as 10th to 23rd

Respondents; in SC/FR/392/2018 as 10th to 23rd Respondents; in SC/FR/07/2019 as

12th to 25th Respondents.

As has already been mentioned above, the 1st Respondent has promoted those

fourteen Inspectors of Police to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police by RTM 855 which

is dated 27-09-2018. However, the Petitioners have filed these cases on the following

dates: the case No. SC/FR/347/2018 on 07-11-2018; the case No. SC/FR/387/2018

on 29-11-2018; the case No. SC/FR/388/2018 on 29-11-2018; the case No.

SC/FR/392/2018 on 04-12-2018; the case No. SC/FR/07/2019 on 11-01-2019. It is

on that basis that the Respondents in the afore-mentioned cases have raised a

Preliminary Objection against the maintainability of this case on the basis that the

Petitioners have failed to file those cases within one month as stipulated in Article

126(2) of the Constitution.

Responding to the above Preliminary Objection, the Petitioners have taken up the

position that they were not aware that these promotions were granted to those

fourteen Inspectors of Police as per the said RTM 855.

However, I observe that RTM 855 is an RTM message which was commonly accessible

to all officers of Petitioners’ ranks. The said message has been addressed to many

people holding various designations in Sri Lanka Police. These include those who are

in charge of all institutions covering all police stations. At this point it is relevant to

recall that it is by an RTM message that the 1st Respondent had called for applications

from officers holding the rank of Inspector of Police to be promoted to the rank of

Chief Inspector of Police. This is RTM No. 655 dated 23-05-2018. It is after seeing that

RTM message that the Petitioners as well as the others had applied for the promotions

responding to the said RTM message. They have applied before the closing date

SC/FR/347/2018 with SC/FR/387/2018, SC/FR/388/2018, SC/FR/392/2018 and SC/FR/07/2019 Page 42 of 42

mentioned in the said message. Furthermore, I observe that all the communications

the Petitioners have relied on for their case are same type of communications; i.e.,

RTM messages. Therefore, it is clear that it is through this type of messages that the

Sri Lanka Police makes its communications with its officers. Thus, I am unable to

accept the fact that the Petitioners had no occasion to see only the RTM 855 dated

27-09-2018 by which the 1st Respondent has promoted those fourteen Inspectors of

Police to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police.

For the above reasons, I am unable to accept the assertion by the Petitioners that

they did not know about the promotions of those fourteen Inspectors of Police to the

rank of Chief Inspector of Police which was effected by the RTM 855 dated 27-09-

2018. Therefore, I have to conclude that the Petitioners have failed to file this case

within one month as stipulated in Article 126(2) of the Constitution. Hence, I proceed

to uphold the Preliminary Objection raised on behalf of the Respondents.

I direct that all the case bearing numbers SC/FR/347/2018, SC/FR/387/2018,

SC/FR/388/2018, SC/FR/392/2018 and SC/FR/07/2019, must stand dismissed without

cost.
