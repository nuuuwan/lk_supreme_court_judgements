Janak De Silva, J.

In this application the Petitioners claimed that that the 9th to 41st Respondents, who are

employees of the 1st Respondent, were granted promotions without calling for

applications contrary to the Scheme of Recruitment and promotions. They further alleged

that the 42nd to 65th Respondents, who are also employees of the 1st Respondent, were

granted salary increments personal to them contrary to regular annual increment

scheme.

Judgment was delivered on 05.08.2025 wherein we held as follows:

(a) The 9th to 41st Respondents were granted promotions and the 42nd to 65th

Respondents were granted salary increments personal to them in an arbitrary

manner based purely on their political affiliation.

(b) The 1st to 7th Respondents has infringed the fundamental rights of the 1st to 8th

Petitioners guaranteed by Article 12(1) of the Constitution.

(c) All promotions granted to the 9th to 41st Respondents and all the salary

increment granted to the 42nd to 65th Respondents consequent to the decision

of the Board of Directors of the 1st Respondent on or about 25.05.2015 are

declared null and void.

(d) The Petitioners are awarded a total of Rs. 4,80,000/= as compensation to be

paid by the 2nd (Nanda Muruththettuwegama), 3rd (Deepika Priyadharshani

Pieris), 4th (W. Jayasiri), 5th (Prof. Keerthi Samarasinghe), 6th (Chandrika

Kulathilaka), and 7th (E. Hettiarachchi) Respondents from their personal funds.

Each of the 1st to 8th Petitioners will get Rs. 60,000/= as compensation. Each of

the 2nd to 7th Respondents will thus pay Rs. 80,000/= each as compensation

from their personal funds.

On or about 05.12.2025, the 34th Respondent filed a petition and affidavit and drew the

attention of Court to the following matters:

1. The original petition filed on 11.08.2015 named him as the 36th Respondent,

2. An amended petition dated 10.09.2015 was filed subsequently and the 36th

Respondent, M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri was removed as a Respondent,

3. The subsequent amended captions dated 19.09.2015, 07.02.2020 and 01.02.2021

did not thereafter name the said M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri as a Respondent,

4. The caption of the judgment delivered on 05.08.2025 included the said M.H.A.M.

Pathmasiri as the 34th Respondent,

5. In terms of the judgment, the 34th Respondent was removed from his position as

Director (Admin & HR) and was placed in the position of Assistant Director (Admin)

by the 1st Respondent,

6. The appointment of the 34th Respondent was not because of a decision of the

Board of Directors of the 1st Respondent on or about 25.05.2015,

7. His appointment was in consequence of a complaint made by him to the Human

Rights Commission of Sri Lanka on 02.07.2013.

Accordingly, the 34th Respondent moved that the caption in the judgment delivered in

this case on 05.08.2025 be amended to exclude the name of M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri.

The motion dated 05.12.2025 filed on behalf of the 34th Respondent was supported in

open Court on 11.02.2026. The Petitioners and the other Respondents who were

represented by counsel informed that they have no objections to the relief sought by the

party cited as 34th Respondent.

I have examined the original case record in this application. As the learned counsel for the

34th Respondent correctly submitted, although the original petition filed on 11.08.2015

named M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri as the 36th Respondent, he was removed as a Respondent in

the amended petition dated 10.09.2015 filed subsequently. Furthermore, the subsequent

amended captions dated 19.09.2015, 07.02.2020 and 01.02.2021 did not name the said

M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri as a Respondent.

In Jeyaraj Fernandopulle v. Premachandra De Silva and Others [(1996) 1 Sri.L.R. 70] a

divisional bench of the Supreme Court held that:

(1) When the Supreme Court has decided a matter, the matter is at an end and there

is no occasion for other judges to be called upon to review or revise a matter. The

Supreme Court is a creature of statute and its powers are statutory. The Court has

no statutory jurisdiction conferred by the Constitution or by any other law to

rehear, review, alter or vary its decision. Decisions of the Supreme Court are final.

(2) As a general rule, no Court has power to rehear, review, alter or vary any judgment

or order made by it after it has been entered. However, all Courts have inherent

power in certain circumstances to revise an order made by them such as a clerical

mistake in a judgment or order or some error arising in a judgment or order from

an accidental slip or omission.

Page 14 of 14

The inclusion of M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri as the 34th Respondent in the caption of the

judgment delivered in this application on 05.08.2025 falls within “clerical mistake or

accidental slip or omission”.

Accordingly, I make order amending the caption of the judgment delivered in this case on

05.08.2025 by excluding the name of the 34th Respondent M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri.

For the avoidance of doubt, the judgment in this application delivered on 05.08.2025 does

not require any change to the post the said M.H.A.M. Pathmasiri held as at 05.08.2025.
