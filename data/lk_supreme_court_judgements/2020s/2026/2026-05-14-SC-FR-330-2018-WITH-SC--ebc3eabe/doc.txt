P. PADMAN SURASENA, CJ

At the commencement of the argument, all parties agreed to conduct a composite

hearing of all connected cases. Hence, this judgment must apply to the cases bearing

numbers SC/FR/330/2018, SC/FR/332/2018 and SC/FR/334/2018.

The 1st Respondent (the then Inspector General of Police) by RTM No. 655 dated 23-

05-2018, called for applications from officers holding the rank of Inspector of Police to

be promoted to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police. The Petitioners have produced

this RTM No. 655 dated 23-05-2018 marked P4 in SC/FR/330/2018; P4 in

SC/FR/332/2018; and P5 in SC/FR/334/2018.

According to the said notice, the officers other than from the Special Task Force and

the Support Services who had the following four qualifications were eligible to apply.

The said four qualifications are as follows:

i. The applicant should be an officer holding the rank of Inspector of Police

ii. The applicant should have been confirmed in the rank of Inspector of Police

iii. The applicant must have had three years satisfactory service as at 01-10-

2017

SC/FR/330/2018 with SC/FR/332/2018 & SC/FR/334/2018 Page 32 of 36

iv. The applicant must have completed eight years active service in the rank of

Inspector of Police by 01-10-2017.

The 10 Petitioners who were then holding the rank of Inspector of Police in Sri Lanka

Police, responding to the notice (RTM No. 655 dated 23-05-2018), had applied for

the afore-said promotions.

Similarly, the fourteen Inspectors of Police whose names are mentioned in the RTM

855 dated 27-09-2018 along with many other Inspectors of Police, had also applied

as per the said notice for this promotion.

The Petitioners have also produced the marking scheme titled “MARKING SCHEME

FOR PROMOTION TO THE RANK OF CHIEF INSPECTOR UNDER THE MERIT +

SENIORITY PROMOTION SCHEME - 2017 (EXCLUDING STF & SUPPORT SERVICES)”.

The Petitioners have marked the said marking scheme as P5 in SC/FR/332/2018 and

P7 SC/FR/334/2018.

Thereafter, the Director Human Resources Management acting on behalf of the

Inspector General of Police by RTM No. 335 dated 10-08-2018 called 120 applicants

for the interviews which was to be held on 15-08-2018 and 17-08-2018. The

Petitioners have produced this RTM No. 335 dated 10-08-2018 marked P7 in

SC/FR/332/2018.

By this time, the Petitioners had previously filed the case bearing No. SC/FR/168/2010

in this Court. Thus, the Petitioners presumably being aggrieved by RTM No. 335 dated

10-08-2018, had obtained a stay order from this Court in the case bearing No.

SC/FR/168/2010 . The Petitioners have produced this stay order dated 06-08-2018

marked P6 in SC/FR/330/2018.

Pursuant to the said stay order, the 1st Respondent has issued RTM 436 suspending

the said promotion proceedings as per the RTM No. 655 dated 23-05-2018. The

SC/FR/330/2018 with SC/FR/332/2018 & SC/FR/334/2018 Page 33 of 36

Petitioners have produced the said RTM 436 dated 13-08-2018 marked P8 in

SC/FR/332/2018; and marked P10 in SC/FR/334/2018.

According to the Petitioners, during the period the afore-said stay order was in force,

the 1st Respondent by RTM 855 dated 27-09-2018 has announced that the National

Police Commission has approved the promotions of the fourteen Inspectors of Police

whose names are mentioned in the said RTM 855, to the rank of Chief Inspector of

Police on supernumerary basis with effect from 02-10-2017. The Petitioners have

produced the said RTM 855 dated 27-09-2018 marked P7 in SC/FR/330/2018; marked

P10 in SC/FR/332/2018; and marked P11 in SC/FR/334/2018.

The Petitioners in their Petitions have stated that they are not aware of the basis on

which the National Police Commission has promoted the said fourteen Inspectors of

Police as per the said RTM 855. The Petitioners in their Petition have also stated that

the promotions of the said fourteen Inspectors of Police as per the said RTM 855 has

been carried out contrary to the afore-mentioned marking scheme.

It is on the above basis that the Petitioners have challenged the promotions to the

rank of Chief Inspector of Police, granted to those fourteen Inspectors of Police as per

the said RTM 855.

The Petitioners in their Petitions have identified those fourteen Inspectors of Police

who were promoted to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police as per the said RTM 855.

In SC/FR/330/2018 they are named as 12th to 25th Respondents; in SC/FR/332/2018

as 12th to 25th Respondents; and in SC/FR/334/2018 as 10th to 23rd Respondents.

Let me at this juncture digress for a moment from the case advanced on behalf of the

Petitioners to the actual sequence of events which had led the 1st Respondent to

announce by RTM 855 dated 27-09-2018 that the National Police Commission has

approved the promotions of the fourteen Inspectors of Police whose names are

mentioned in the said RTM 855, to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police on

supernumerary basis.

The Petitioners as well as the Respondents whose promotions the Petitioners have

sought to challenge in these Applications are officers who had initially joined the Sri

Lanka Reserve Police Force as Sub Inspectors of Police.

The Respondents whose promotions the Petitioners have sought to challenge in these

Applications, having fulfilled the required merits were thereafter absorbed into Regular

Police Force as Sub Inspectors of Police. These Respondents are persons who have

initially joined as Sub Inspectors of Sri Lanka Reserve Police in 1989 and 1990. After

they were absorbed into Regular Police Force, they continued to engage in various

duties as Sub Inspectors of Police of Sri Lanka Regular Police. Although the Petitioners

have not clearly set out the basis on which they were absorbed into Regular Police

Force it may be that the Petitioners also were persons who were absorbed into Regular

Police Force on merit. Be that as it may, one thing is clear: that is the fact that the

Respondents whose promotions the Petitioners have sought to challenge in these

Applications had been absorbed into Sri Lanka Police Regular Force on merit by 13-

05-2002.2 (In the same paragraph these Respondents have stated that to the best of

their knowledge the Petitioners are also persons who were absorbed into Regular

Cadre during the same period.

However, those who failed to secure their absorption into Regular Cadre on merits as

they failed to meet the required standard on merits remained in the Sri Lanka Reserve

Police Force and continued to work as Reserve Sub Inspectors of Police. As the

promotion process within Sri Lanka Reserve Police Force was comparatively easier and

less competitive than Sri Lanka Police Regular Force, those who failed to secure their

absorption into Regular Cadre on merits received promotions as Reserve Inspectors of

Police somewhere in 1998 and Reserve Chief Inspectors of Police somewhere in 2002.

The fortune for those who failed to secure their absorption into Regular Cadre on

merits came in 2006 when the Cabinet of Ministers decided to abolish Sri Lanka

Reserve Police Force with effect from 01-02-2006 and absorb all the officers in Sri

Lanka Reserve Police Force into the same ranks in Sri Lanka Police Regular Force. This

led to a situation where those who failed to secure their absorption into Regular Cadre

on merits during the period in which the Petitioners secure their absorption into

Regular Cadre on merits, being placed in the seniority list of officers in Sri Lanka Police

Regular Force very much above those who made it on merit by 13-05-2002 and

became Sub Inspectors of Police Regular Force. Thus, those who secured their

absorption into Regular Cadre on merits by 13-05-2002, continued to be Sub

Inspectors of Police Regular Force even at the time those who failed to secure their

absorption into Regular Cadre on merits at that time overnight becoming Inspectors

of Police and Chief Inspectors of Police with effect from 01-02-2006; this was with the

decision of the Cabinet of Ministers to abolish Sri Lanka Reserve Police Force.

Thereafter, complaining this injustice those Respondents started agitating their

grievances before various fora including the Human Rights Commission, this Court,

Inspector General of Police and the National Police Commission. Having considered

their appeals in this regard, the National Police Commission had decided to promote

these Respondents to the ranks of Chief Inspector of Police on a supernumerary basis

with effect from 02-10-2017. It was that decision which the RTM 855 dated 27-09-

2018 had notified to the others.

There is no evidence that the Petitioners had agitated their grievances if they had

belonged to the same category and had the same grievance as that of the

Respondents. The case the petitioners advanced is that the promotions of those

Respondents were carried out contrary to the process of calling for applications from

officers holding the rank of Inspector of Police to be promoted to the rank of Chief

Inspector of Police by RTM No. 655 (P4) dated 23-05-2018 initiated by the Inspector

General of Police. Furthermore, the position of the Petitioners is that they are not

aware of the basis the 1st Respondent has promoted 12th to 25th Respondents. They

merely pray that the promotions granted to the 10th to 25th Respondents to the rank

of Chief Inspector of Police be quashed on the basis that they are outside the process

initiated by the Inspector General of Police as per RTM No. 655 (P4) dated 23-05-

2018. I am unable to agree with the Petitioners that the act of granting promotions to

the 10th to 25th Respondents to the rank of Chief Inspector of Police at this instance

by the National Police Commission as an arbitrary act without any justification. Indeed,

the National Police Commission has acted at this instance to remedy an injustice

caused to these Respondents. Since the Petitioners have not proved that they too had

belonged to the same category or they had the same grievance as that of the

Respondents or they had agitated a similar grievance as that of those Respondents, I

am unable to hold that the Petitioners are similarly circumstanced as these

Respondents. If they are by any chance, then they have slept over their rights.

Therefore, I have to conclude that the Petitioners are not entitled to succeed with their

Applications.

I direct that all the cases bearing numbers SC/FR/330/2018, SC/FR/332/2018 and

SC/FR/334/2018 must stand dismissed without costs.
